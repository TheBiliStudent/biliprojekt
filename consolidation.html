Consolidation

At first the Allied powers worked together to achieve them. But as time went on it became apparent, that their ideological differences were too big. Nonetheless the occupying forces supervised the development of the young German states. Examples would be the AAC or Marshall Plan.

The Allied Control Council (AAC) was a temporary government. It replaced the Nazi government in Germany after the second world war.

The Marshall Plan was an economic plan with political interests behind it. The US would support any country which dedicated themselves to western democracy. Through this plan the FRG was able to regain self-esteem and build itself up again. Economic stability played a role in the stability of the whole democracy, since the people have an easier time to accept a system if they gain a better life because of it.

This was crucial for the short term as well as the long-term development for the country and was only possible because they learned from history.

The constitutions were based on the Weimar Republic’s, leaning from their mistakes.

The Allied powers saw the mistakes made in the treatment of Germany after World War I. They did not want to destroy Germany but recognised they could give Germany a second chance to learn under the supervision and with the aid of them.
