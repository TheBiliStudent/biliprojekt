The GDR’s constitution

There were three separate official constitution in the state of East Germany. One each from 1949, 1968 and 1974.

The first constitution was created and approved by the German People’s Congress. It was heavily inspired by the Weimar constitution, this influence being similar to the Grundgesetzt. It was intended to have a united Germany in mind.

The constitution introduced two chambers, a “Länderkammer” and a “Volkeskammer”.  Basic rights and  were also put into the written text of the constitution.

The constitution left the possibility for a unified Germany or a socialist State.

Over time the SED slowly change certain aspects as holding elections via one of the SEDs organisations.

After Walter Ulbricht became chairman, a new constitution was created to be more socialist. Their goal was to become a socialist society.

It said that the government should consist of workers in the SED.

The last change was made in 1974. The new constitution left out the possibility for a unified Germany as the GDR government wanted to create an individual national identity.

A change to the constitution was made after the peaceful revolution, changing it to be less communist and deleting the passage which gave the SED all the power.
