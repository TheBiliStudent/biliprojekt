Democratisation

After Germany was defeated and the allied powers took over governing Germany, the second phase of the transformation process could start.

At the Potsdam conference the big three fleshed out their plans on new Germany. They agreed on the five Ds.

Denazification

All allied forces saw Denazification as an important aspect to transform Germany. The goal was to make the new state’s democracy defensive and combative, as well as having Germany not trouble the world order again. For that they abolished and forbid Nazi related organisations.

Next to this they held the Nuremberg trials not only to punish the old leaders and organisations but also to be show trials. They were charged with crimes against peace, war crimes and crimes against humanity. The trials were recorded, translated and shown to the rest of the world. The goals behind this were to not only show what crimes happened in Germany but also having fair trials, as not everyone was convicted.

A second part to denazification was reeducation, the plan to have Germany people believe in democracy and supervise the education of children change from the nazi teachings.

Demilitarisation

The German military was to be abolished and any industry that could produce weaponry with it. It was supposed to go along with the deindustrialization.

Deindustrialisation

The Morgenthau plan was an idea to completely deindustrialises Germany to agricultural state. Before the end of the war, Henry Morgenthau Jr. proposed this plan for a post war Germany. It was never put into action as the allied powers preferred to dismantle most of Germany’s industry but kept mines intact for production. In 1947 the US changed course, instead wanting to stabilise Germany as a potential trading partner.

Decentralisation

In the time before power in Germany had become more centralised. The allied forced wanted to prevent a centralised power from taking over again. The democratic system in the western occupation zones were build “from the ground up” separated into local governments, then the “Bundesländer” and finally a central government. At first the German people were supposed to elect local governments, since they are local, and it would make the German people feel the effects of democracy.

Democratisation
